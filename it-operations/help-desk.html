Our Help Desk Support Process

Every ticket incoming to our IT help desk progresses as follows:

1

Creation

A user reports an issue via one of our support channels (email, phone, etc.), and the corresponding ticket is created automatically or by a support agent.

2

Processing

The ticket is categorized according to the issue type and urgency and assigned to the appropriate support engineer. Usually, this step is automated for L1 tickets and performed manually for L2–L3 tickets due to their complexity.

3

Resolution and escalation

The assigned support agent resolves the ticket or escalates it to the next level (if the issue requires more in-depth expertise).

4

User notification

The user is notified about their ticket's changed status and resolution.

5

Post-resolution analysis and improvement suggestions

The team analyzes the incident more deeply, identifies the root cause, and suggests improvements to prevent recurrence.

6

Continuous optimization of help desk workflows

The team together with the team lead and project manager (optional) monitor KPIs, analyze user feedback, review the help desk processes, and identify improvement opportunities.

KPIs to Keep the Help Desk in Good Health

Below are the fundamental KPIs we track during our help desk engagements.

First response time (FRT)

i.e., the time it takes for a support agent to respond to a user request.

FRT to strive for: ≤30 min (for emails), ≤40 sec (for calls).

User satisfaction rate (USAT)

USAT to strive for: ≥90%.

Resolution rate (RR)

i.e., the ratio of resolved tickets to the total number of tickets received within a specific period.

RR to strive for: ≥70%.

Still, the SLA can cover the tracking of other metrics, including the average time to resolution, call abandonment rate, mean time to engineer.
